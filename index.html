About Annelise



Who is Annelise?

I'm a Seattle-area native and optimistic people-person. I'm a daughter, sister, class clown, user advocate, knitting extrordinaire, and ukulele player with strong opinions on the local Seattle ice cream scene. I think LeBron James is better than Michael Jordan, but I'm open to being convinced otherwise.

Why UX Design?

I'm genuinely fascinated by understanding human behavior and invested in helping to enhance the wellbeing of people in my community. I value building genuine connections with other people and believe it is important to use my talents and skills to serve marginalized and under-served communities. It's this value that has guided me from a professional background in Health Promotion Research and Social Work to my current career in User Experience Research and Design.

When working with at-risk youth and families, I observed first-hand the power that technology (specifically mobile apps) had in their lives. In fact, parents would often tell me that mobile games and social media apps were a regular part of their self-care routines. As access to the internet increases, I saw web-based services and apps break barriers to access in ways that traditional programs have often failed to do.

Because of this, it seemed inevitable that I needed to get into UX design in order to serve these communities on a larger scale. I'm excited about exploring the power that design can have in people's lives and am always searching for new and improved ways to reach them.
