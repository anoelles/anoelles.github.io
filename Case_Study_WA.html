Washington State Employment Security Department (ESD)

Designing a mobile app to make the unemployment claims process inclusive and user-friendly.

PROJECT BACKGROUND

What is WA ESD? The Employment Security Department helps Washington stateâ€™s workers and employers succeed in the global economy by delivering employment services and an unemployment-benefits system. Today, Washington State residents can file for unemployment insurance benefits by telephone or online. There are no in-person unemployment offices in Washington state. The fastest way to apply for benefits is online. Currently, 46% of all initial applications are made over the phone, however records show that 94% of all claims could have been completed online.

Vision: The WA ESD mobile application will provide job seekers with an efficient and user-friendly way to file their weekly claim, log their required job search efforts, and seek out assistance via an up to date FAQ section. Job Seekers will feel at ease with a clearly structured process, and a friendly yet secure interface.

Research

What we gathered: 13 user interviews, scholarly journal articles, government statistics and data, other state government apps

We needed to gather more information on the current unemployment claims process, so we partnered with the local WorkSource office in Seattle, WA to interview clients who were currently on unemployment. We used our own social networks and snowball sampling to identify and interview other people about their experience on unemployment.

Next, we incorporated research on unemployment demographics and smartphone use.

Using this information, we created an affinity diagram to pull out the main themes and user needs.

Themes

Through our analysis and affinity diagramming, we came up with core themes to guide our design. We also paired each of these themes with an associated design heuristic.

Simplicity

Users wanted to process to be more simple to use than the complicated forms currently provided.

Efficiency

Users had to re-enter the same information over and over again (such as address, phone number) even when nothing had changed.

Mobility

Many users wanted said they would love to be able to file their claim while on public transportation or enter a job activity right after an interview while still at the employer location.

Personal Touch

Users said that the current process feels impersonal and that they feel like a number rather than a person. They wanted a more personalized experience.

Respect

The poor design on the current site and user flow left people feeling disrespected. Users wanted a design that looked like they cared about the process and the users themselves.

Support

Users identified feeling like they weren't supported by the current site and process, thus making them lose fatih in the services themselves.

Design and Testing

After defining our goals and criteria based on user initial user research, we did ALOT of sketching. We used the design studio process to generate solutions to specific user-identified pain points and used paper-prototyping to get rapid feedback on our early designs.

Our main focus was to keep the research and personas in mind for every design decision.

Example:Users stated that they would most likely be using the app on pubic transit, so we made the radio buttons and other features larger to accomodate the movement of being in vehicle.
