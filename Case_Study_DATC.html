Dive Around the Clock Website Re-design

THE CHALLENGE

Design a cohesive brand identity for non-profit Dive Around the Clock and communicate that brand through a new website that motivates new divers and donors to get involved with their event and oragnization.

PROJECT BACKGROUND: Dive Around the Clock (DATC) is a Non-Profit community of Scuba Divers and Volunteers from the Seattle-area that come together once a year for a 24-hour underwater Dive-a-Thon relay. The purpose of the event is to raise funds for cancer research at Seattle Children's Hospital.

DATC has grown since it started 10 years ago into a fully-functioning non-profit. They have never had much of an online presence, as many of the divers were recruited to their event through word-of-mouth at local dive shops. After a recent leadership change, the new management of DATC felt that they needed "a better website" to achieve their goals of (1) recruiting divers and volunteers and (2) raising money through individual donations and corporate sponsorships.

VISION: Create a new, cohesive brand identity for Dive Around the Clock that better communicates their mission, expands their community, and allows users to easily join the DATC cause.

TEAM: Annelise Smith, Thomas Roberts, Simi Sood

Research

To learn more about potential divers/volunteers/donors and gain a better understanding of issues with the current site, we conducted an online survey, one-on-one interviews, usability testing, a comparative analysis, and a heuristic evaluation of the original site.

SURVEY AND INTERVIEWS: We created a survey to send to the members of the DATC community to identify how members got involved in Dive Around the Clock, why they dive, and their feelings about the current site. We received responses from 8 of 16 members, most expressing the need for better navigation and the ability to sign-up to dive, donate, and/or volunteer for the event.

USABILITY TESTING: We completed usability tests with 12 users to identify pain points and navigational issues. Users were asked to complete 4 tasks:

  • 1. Understand what Dive Around the Clock is based on the home page (0% completion)
  • 2. Sign-up to be a diver at the event (75% completion)
  • 3. Sign-up to volunteer for the event (100% completion)
  • 4. Donate money to DATC (100% completion)

HEURISTIC EVALUATION: I went through the current site and evaluated it based on Nielson's 10 Usability Heuristics. The areas where the current site had major issues were in the areas of Flexibility/Efficiency of Use and Aesthetic Minimlaist Design.

Analysis

From our research, we identified pain points with the current site and used affinity mapping to idenitfy divers' and volunteers' needs and motivations. This helped us develop personas and a style/tone guide that would focus our re-design efforts.

PAIN POINTS: Most users were able to complete the "donate", "dive", and "volunteer" tasks. However, not a single user could identify what the organization, event, or cause were. This was a huge deterent, as users didn't feel motivated to complete any tasks if they didn't know what they were even looking at on the home page.

AFFINITY DIAGRAMMING: From our interviews, we wanted to get an understanding of what motivates people to dive, donate, or volunteer for a cause or event. We also incorporated behavioral research from academic sources on what motivates people to get involved in charity events. After pulling out these themes, we tried to incorporate them into the messaging and tone of the website.

Design and Testing

We did TONS of sketching to create our initial designs, then created paper-prototypes, wireframes, and hi-fi clickable prototypes to incorporate user feedback at all stages to iterate and refine our intial ideas.

Research Informed Design Decisions

For our final design, I kept the research in mind and made design decisions based on actual user information and behavior

EXAMPLE 1: During our usability testing, none of the users could identify what DATC was. In our newly designed homepage, we created the tagline "Suit Up, Dive In, Fight Cancer" to entice users to read on and made all of the important information available without scrolling or clicking around.
