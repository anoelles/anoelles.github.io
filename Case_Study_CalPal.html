CalPal Alexa Skill

THE CHALLENGE:


Design and deploy an Alexa skill that helps enhance user wellness and supports behavior that helps users meet health and wellbeing goals.

PROJECT BACKGROUND: General Assembly hosted an Alexa Hackathon where UX Designers and Web Developers had 8 hours to design an Alexa Skill. The theme of the hackathon was to develop an Alexa skill that would help enhance the user's wellbeing. We had 30 minutes of brainstorming to pitch ideas and form teams, then 7.5 hours to build our skill.

VISION: Create a skill that helps the user better understand how much exercise is required to burn off a certain amount of calories. By making it easier to conceptualize the amount of calories consumed in terms of workout time, users can make healthier eating decisions and reach fitness/weight goals.

During initial planning, we came up with these additional requirements/contraints:

-Must be fully functioning in 8 hours
-No data mining/scraping available
-No database building available
-Must enhance user wellbeing

TEAM:3 web developers and 4 UX Designers

TOOLS: Pen & Paper, Whiteboard, Amazon Developer Console, AWS Management Console, Adobe Illustrator

TIME LIMIT: 8 hours

DELIVERABLES:deployable Alexa skill, branding and logo, final presentation

Research

I completed user interviews and a survey to better understand users' current eating and exercise habits and the context in which they would use our skill. I also incorporated health promotion and motivational interviewing research to help guide the language CalPal would use when interacting with users.

INTERVIEWS AND SURVEY: We kept the research directed, quick, and no-cost. I led the development of a survey and an interview script. Participants were recruited from other Hackathon teams and I used my background in field/guerilla marketing to successfully recruit participants from the general public outside our building.

SECONDARY RESEARCH: Drawing on my professional experience in health promotion, I consulted articles on evidence-based motivational interviewing techniques used by health professionals when working with clients who are seeking to change health behaviors. We hoped to incorporate this into the language used by our skill.

Analysis

From our research, I worked with my team to create a realistic context scenario and identify results of our interviews/survey to be incorporated in the way our skill would interact with users.


CONTEXT SCENARIO: A majority of users reported that they would use this after an unhealthy eating choice was made (rather than before). These situations usually involved going out to eat and drink with friends or coworkers and consuming too much.


Users reported feeling the following in this scenario:
-guilt about their poor eating decision
-determination to make up for poor eating decision
-desire to get back on track with health goals


SURVEY RESULTS: Walking was the most common activity reported, followed by running, biking, and swimming. Survey showed that users would prefer to have calories converions reported in time units (minutes, hours) rather than distance (miles, km).



Research-Informed Design

I did more user testing to understand the ways users might ask CalPal for information, then worked with the developers to make sure CalPal would respond in a clear and natural way.


NATURAL LANGUAGE: Through brainstorming and user testing, we identified the possible phrases users might naturally use to ask CalPal for information. Working with developers, we created utterances that CalPal would respond to that were congruent with this natural language.


ERROR PREVENTION: Error prevention and messages are especially important when interacting with voice recognition technology. Through user testing, we identified possible errors, then crafted responses for these error states that incorporated techniques from motivational interviewing research to minimize frustration and encourage the user to achieve their goal.


Error example: User asks CalPal to calculate calorie information for an activity that is not currently programmed into CalPal.

Before User Testing-
User: How long would I have to ice skate to burn 500 calories?
CalPal: I'm not sure about that activity. Could you ask again?

After User Testing-
User: How long would I have to ice skate to burn 500 calories?
CalPal: I'm not sure about that activity, but your could run for about 1 hour to burn 500 calories.

Branding

We incorporated themes from user research and secondary health promotion research
to create develop a name that was friendly, supportive, and non-judgemental.



BRANDING AND VOICE: Research has shown that interactions with health professionals and counselors that promote acceptance, rapport-building, and positioning oneself as an equal partner (rather than as an authority figure) work best at facilitating behavior change (learn more here).

Incorporating these themes, we named our skill CalPal. We wanted users to feel that CalPal was more like a friend rather than an authority figure and that CalPal was a non-judgemental "pal"


ICON DEVELOPMENT: I worked with our visual designer to create an icon for CalPal that incorporated these themes and reflected the scenario that users would mostly be using it in (i.e. burning off calories after overeating with friends).

The Result

CalPal won 1st place in the Hackathon and is now a fully deployable Alexa skill.
