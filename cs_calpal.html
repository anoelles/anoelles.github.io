CalPal // Alexa Skill

Design and deploy an Alexa skill that helps enhance user wellness and supports behavior that helps users meet health and wellbeing goals.

Project Background

An Alexa Skill?

Alexa (the Amazon voice service behind millions of devices including the Amazon Echo) provides capabilities, or skills, that enable customers to create a more personalized experience. Amazon provides an Alexa Skills Kit (ASK) which is a collection of self-service APIs, tools, documentation, and code samples that enable developers to build and host most skills for free using Amazon Web Services.

About the Hackathon

General Assembly hosted an Alexa Hackathon where UX Designers and Web Developers had 8 hours to design an Alexa Skill. The theme of the hackathon was to develop an Alexa skill that would help enhance the user's wellbeing.

We had 30 minutes of brainstorming to pitch ideas and form teams, then 7.5 hours to build our skill. During initial planning, we came up with these additional requirements/contraints:

-Must be fully functioning in 8 hours
-No data mining/scraping available
-No database-building available

Tools

Pen & Paper, Whiteboard, Amazon Developer Console, AWS Management Console, Adobe Illustrator

Deliverables

Fully-deployable Alexa skill, branding and logo, final presentation with UX research summary

My Roles

I designed and implemented our user research strategy which included surveys, semi-structured interviews, and a literature review of scholarly articles. I also led persona development and created context scenarios and user flows to guide our developers. Working with our visual designer, I helped come up with the logo and branding guidelines. Lastly, my team elected to have me put together and deliver our stakeholder presentation.

Team

Research

I completed user interviews and a survey to better understand users' current eating and exercise habits and the context in which they would use our skill. I also incorporated health promotion and motivational interviewing research to help guide the language CalPal would use when interacting with users.



Surveys and Interviews

We kept the research directed, quick, and no-cost. I led the development of a survey and an interview script. Participants were recruited from other Hackathon teams and I used my background in field/guerilla marketing to successfully recruit participants from the general public outside our building.

Secondary Research

Drawing on my professional experience in health promotion, I consulted articles on evidence-based motivational interviewing techniques used by health professionals when working with clients who are seeking to change health behaviors. We hoped to incorporate this into the language used by our skill.

Analysis

From our research, I worked with my team to create a realistic context scenario and identify results of our interviews/survey to be incorporated in the way our skill would interact with users.



Context Scenario

A majority of users reported that they would use this after an unhealthy eating choice was made (rather than before). These situations usually involved going out to eat and drink with friends or coworkers and consuming too much.

Users reported feeling the following in this scenario:
-guilt about their poor eating decision
-determination to make up for poor eating decision
-desire to get back on track with health goals

Survey Results

Walking was the most common activity reported, followed by running, biking, and swimming. Survey showed that users would prefer to have calories converions reported in time units (minutes, hours) rather than distance (miles, km).

Research-Informed Design

I did more user testing to understand the ways users might ask CalPal for information, then worked with the developers to make sure CalPal would respond in a clear and natural way.



Natural Language

Through brainstorming and user testing, we identified the possible phrases users might naturally use to ask CalPal for information. Working with developers, we created utterances that CalPal would respond to that were congruent with this natural language.

Error Prevention

Error prevention and messages are especially important when interacting with voice recognition technology. Through user testing, we identified possible errors, then crafted responses for these error states that incorporated techniques from motivational interviewing research to minimize frustration and encourage the user to achieve their goal.

Branding

We incorporated themes from user research and secondary health promotion research to create develop a name that was friendly, supportive, and non-judgemental.

Branding and Voice


Research has shown that interactions with health professionals and counselors that promote acceptance, rapport-building, and positioning oneself as an equal partner (rather than as an authority figure) work best at facilitating behavior change (learn more here). Incorporating these themes, we named our skill CalPal: a non-judgemental "pal" rather than an authority figure.

I worked with our visual designer to create an icon for CalPal that incorporated these themes and reflected the user scenario (i.e. burning off calories after overeating with friends).

The Solution


CalPal won 1st place in the Hackathon and is now a fully deployable Alexa skill.
